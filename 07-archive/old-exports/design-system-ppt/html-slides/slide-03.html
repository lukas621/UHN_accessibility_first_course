SECTION 02 · HEALTHCARE RELEVANCE

Why this matters in healthcare

Accessibility is not a separate program. It is the difference between a patient feeling seen and a patient leaving without the care they came for.

Drop a reception-scene photo

1 in 4

Canadian adults aged 15+ live with one or more disabilities. That is a quarter of the patients, families, and colleagues you serve every shift.

Most accessibility decisions at UHN are made not in policy meetings -- they are made at reception desks, in exam rooms, during phone intakes, and in the brief seconds before a person speaks.

Foundations matter because they make those split-second decisions consistent, dignified, and legally sound.

KEY TAKEAWAY

Every interaction at UHN is an accessibility decision -- whether we name it that or not.
