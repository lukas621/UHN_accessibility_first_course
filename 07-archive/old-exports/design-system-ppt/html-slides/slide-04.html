SECTION 03 · A BIGGER DEFINITION

Accessibility is more than ramps and railings

Physical access is the most visible barrier -- but most of what UHN gets wrong every day is invisible until we look for it.

CONCEPT

Accessibility is the removal of any barrier that keeps a person from full, dignified participation in care.

Barriers can be physical, but they can also be attitudinal, communication-based, technological, sensory, or systemic. Every barrier removed widens the door for someone.

PHYSICAL

Doors, paths, seating, exam tables, parking.

COMMUNICATION

Plain language, signage, formats, interpretation.

ATTITUDINAL

Assumptions, tone, body language, deference.

SYSTEMIC

Forms, scheduling, intake, signage, policy.

HEALTHCARE EXAMPLE

Drop a detail photo

A patient is late to an appointment. The barrier isn't mobility. It is an appointment reminder sent only as an image -- unreadable by the screen reader they rely on.

Fixing the building doesn't fix the visit. Fixing the message does.
