The Accessibility in Practice Model

A shared model that lets every team -- clinical, administrative, operational, and leadership -- talk about accessibility in the same language.

01 Awareness

Recognize that a person may have visible or invisible accessibility needs -- without making them ask first.

  • Scan environments and forms.
  • Notice assistive devices.
  • Watch your own assumptions.

02 Communication

Use plain, respectful language and confirm the person's preferred way to receive information.

  • Ask about preferred format.
  • Speak to the patient, not the companion.
  • Slow down when needed.

03 Environment

Adapt the space so the person can move, see, hear, and rest without unnecessary effort.

  • Clear paths and seating.
  • Lighting and signage.
  • Quiet space if needed.

04 Response

Adjust your actions in the moment, document what worked, and pass the learning forward.

  • Offer -- don't impose -- help.
  • Confirm what worked.
  • Update the chart or note.

REFLECTION

The four areas are not a checklist. They are four lenses you can move between in any interaction -- sometimes within the same minute.
