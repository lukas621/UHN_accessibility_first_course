## Layer: Base (Scenario Setup)

**File:** `voiceover_1.9_base.mp3`
**Trigger:** Auto-play on slide load
**Tone:** Storytelling, thoughtful

(Beat — 1.5 seconds)

Here is one more scenario — and this one is about how teams work together.

(Beat — 1 second)

Your team uses an accessibility checklist when supporting patients. A colleague mentions that a patient who speaks Cantonese and has a cognitive disability seemed confused during intake, even though the checklist was completed. Your colleague says, "We followed the checklist — I am not sure what else we can do."

(Beat — 2 seconds)

Think about this carefully. The checklist was completed. The process was followed. But the patient was still confused. What does that tell you?

Choose from the three options on screen.

**[104 words | ~42 seconds]**

## Layer: Choice A — Best Response

**File:** `voiceover_1.9_choiceA.mp3`
**Trigger:** On selection of Choice A
**Tone:** Affirming, thoughtful

You chose to acknowledge the checklist is a starting point, but suggest the team explore what barriers the patient actually experienced — language support, cognitive load, cultural context — and adapt the process.

(Beat — 1 second)

This is the best response. The team begins to see accessibility as more than a checklist. They plan to involve interpretation services, simplify intake materials, and consider cultural context. You have modelled the Awareness area of the Accessibility in Practice model — recognizing that needs may not be covered by a standard process.

**[80 words | ~32 seconds]**

## Layer: Choice B — Acceptable Response

**File:** `voiceover_1.9_choiceB.mp3`
**Trigger:** On selection of Choice B
**Tone:** Balanced, constructive

You chose to suggest requesting an interpreter for next time.

(Beat — 1 second)

This addresses one barrier — language. But the patient also has a cognitive disability, and there may be cultural dimensions the team has not explored. An interpreter helps with language, but simplified forms, visual aids, or adjusted pacing might be needed for the cognitive component.

Think about intersectionality — people often experience multiple barriers at once.

**[62 words | ~25 seconds]**

## Layer: Choice C — Poor Response

**File:** `voiceover_1.9_choiceC.mp3`
**Trigger:** On selection of Choice C
**Tone:** Gentle but direct

You chose to agree the checklist was completed and move on.

(Beat — 1 second)

This is a missed opportunity. The checklist is a tool, not a guarantee. When a patient is confused despite a completed checklist, that is a signal — not a pass. The patient's experience does not improve. The team misses a chance to learn and grow.

Remember: accessibility requires awareness, curiosity, and flexibility beyond any checklist.
