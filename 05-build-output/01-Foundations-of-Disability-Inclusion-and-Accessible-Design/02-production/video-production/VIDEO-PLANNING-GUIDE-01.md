### Captioning Notes
- Closed captions must be provided in SRT or VTT format
- Captions on by default in Storyline player
- Caption text: white on semi-transparent black background, minimum 18pt equivalent
- Include speaker identification if multiple voices
- Include non-speech audio descriptions in brackets: [upbeat music], [door opening sound]
- Canadian English spelling throughout

### Audio Description Track
- Optional audio description track for learners who are blind or have low vision
- Describes visual-only content: "A wide shot of a modern hospital entrance with an accessible ramp and automatic doors. Diverse staff members walk through the lobby."

### AI Generation Option
If producing with AI video tools:
- Use a virtual presenter that reflects UHN's diverse workforce (not a generic stock avatar)
- Ensure lip sync is accurate for Canadian English
- B-roll can be sourced from stock footage libraries (search: "Canadian hospital," "accessible healthcare," "diverse healthcare workers Toronto")
- Review AI-generated video for: accurate disability representation, natural movement, no uncanny valley effects
- Tools: Synthesia, HeyGen, or D-ID for presenter; NanoBanana or Midjourney for still-to-video b-roll frames; Runway for motion

## Video 2: Scenario — Marcus at the Reception Desk (Optional)

### Overview

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Title** | Scenario: Marcus and the Interpreter |
| **Duration** | 60-90 seconds |
| **Style** | Dramatic scenario — live-action preferred, AI-generated acceptable |
| **Screen Placement** | Screen 7 — Scenario 1 |
| **AI Generation Candidate** | Partial — AI can generate the setup scene; human actors preferred for emotional nuance |

### Script

---

**[OPEN — Medium shot of a hospital reception desk. Lin, the receptionist, is typing at her computer.]**

NARRATOR (voiceover):

"Marcus is 45 years old and Deaf. He communicates in American Sign Language. He has arrived at a UHN outpatient clinic for a pre-surgical consultation."

**[Marcus approaches the desk. He holds up his phone showing a text message: "I need a sign language interpreter for my appointment."]**

NARRATOR:

"His appointment is in 20 minutes. There is no interpreter on site. Lin has not encountered this situation before."

**[Close-up of Lin's face — she looks uncertain, not hostile. She glances at her computer, then back at Marcus.]**

NARRATOR:

"What should Lin do? Think about the Accessibility Decision Path: Pause, Listen, Apply, Adapt, Seek Support."

**[FREEZE FRAME on the scene. Text overlay appears: "What would you do?" with three options listed.]**

NARRATOR:

"Choose your response on screen."

**[FADE TO: Interactive branching in Storyline]**

---

### Shot List

| Shot # | Type | Description | Duration |
|--------|------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Medium | Reception desk, Lin working | 4s |
| 2 | Wide | Marcus approaching the desk | 4s |
| 3 | Close-up | Phone screen showing text message | 3s |
| 4 | Medium two-shot | Marcus and Lin at the desk | 5s |
| 5 | Close-up | Lin's uncertain expression | 3s |
| 6 | Freeze frame | Scene freezes, text overlay appears | 5s |

### Captioning Notes
- All narration captioned in full
- Marcus does not speak — his communication is via phone text (shown visually on screen and described in narration)
- Do not caption Marcus's facial expressions as "confused" or "frustrated" — remain neutral
- Caption the text on his phone: [Phone screen reads: "I need a sign language interpreter for my appointment."]

### Sensitivity Review
- Marcus should be portrayed as calm and self-advocating, not distressed
- Lin should be portrayed as uncertain but willing — not dismissive or rude
- A Deaf consultant should review the final video for accurate representation
- Do not use background music during Marcus's scenes (respect for Deaf culture — the scene should not rely on audio mood-setting that excludes the character's experience)

## General Production Guidelines

### Accessibility Requirements (All Videos)

| Requirement | Standard | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Closed captions | WCAG 2.1 AA | On by default in Storyline. SRT/VTT format. |
| Caption accuracy | 99%+ | Review all auto-generated captions manually. |
| Audio description | WCAG 2.1 AA | Required for videos with visual-only information. |
| Transcript | Full text transcript | Available as downloadable document for each video. |
| Flashing content | WCAG 2.1 AA | No content flashes more than 3 times per second. |
| Colour contrast | 4.5:1 minimum | Text overlays on video must meet contrast requirements. |
| Auto-play | Disabled | Videos must not auto-play. Learner initiates playback. |
| Playback controls | Visible, keyboard-accessible | Play, pause, volume, captions toggle, full screen. |

### File Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Resolution | 1280x720 (720p) |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 |
| Codec | H.264 |
| Format | MP4 |
| Frame rate | 30fps |
| Audio | AAC, 128kbps minimum, mono or stereo |
| Max file size | 50MB per video (for Storyline embedding) |

### Brand Compliance

- Title cards use UHN brand colours: navy #192858 background, white text
- UHN logo appears on opening and closing title cards only (not throughout)
- "Accessibility First" series branding appears on title cards
- Font: Arial Black for titles, Arial for body text in overlays
- All text overlays: minimum 24pt equivalent at 1280x720

### Review Process

1. Script review by accessibility SME
2. Draft video review for disability representation accuracy
3. Caption review (human review of any auto-generated captions)
4. Audio description review (if applicable)
5. Deaf/hard-of-hearing community review for Scenario 1 (Marcus)
6. Final approval by course owner
