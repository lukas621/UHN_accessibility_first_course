## 2. Course Goal

This course establishes a shared language and foundational understanding of disability, inclusion, and accessible design so that all UHN employees can recognize barriers — attitudinal, systemic, communication, digital, and policy-based — and begin applying proactive, rights-based approaches to accessibility in their daily work.

---

## 3. Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

| CLO | Learning Outcome | Bloom's Level | Assessment Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| CLO 1 | Define accessibility as encompassing attitudinal, systemic, communication, digital, and policy barriers — not only physical barriers — within healthcare settings. | Understand (Level 2) | Multiple-choice knowledge check; scenario-based identification activity |
| CLO 2 | Explain the key legislative foundations of the *Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act* (AODA) and the *Ontario Human Rights Code* (OHRC) as they apply to UHN's obligations and everyday practice. | Understand (Level 2) | Drag-and-drop matching activity linking legislation to workplace obligations |
| CLO 3 | Analyze how disability intersects with race, gender, age, income, and language to create compounded barriers in healthcare delivery. | Analyze (Level 4) | Scenario-based reflection with guided prompts; intersectionality case analysis |
| CLO 4 | Differentiate between a reactive accommodation model and a proactive, governance-based approach to accessible design. | Analyze (Level 4) | Compare-and-contrast scenario activity; decision-path interaction |
| CLO 5 | Identify personal and team actions that position accessibility as a shared organizational responsibility at UHN. | Apply (Level 3) | My Action Planning (MAP) activity; commitment-based reflection |

## 4. Sub-Learning Outcomes

### CLO 1: Define accessibility beyond physical barriers

- 1.1 Identify at least three categories of barriers (attitudinal, systemic, communication, digital, policy) that people with disabilities encounter in healthcare environments.
- 1.2 Distinguish between the medical model, social model, and rights-based model of disability.
- 1.3 Recognize accessibility as a human right, not a discretionary service enhancement.

### CLO 2: Explain key legislative foundations (AODA, OHRC)

- 2.1 Summarize the purpose and scope of the AODA and its relevance to UHN as a designated public sector organization in Ontario.
- 2.2 Describe UHN's obligations under the OHRC, including the duty to accommodate to the point of undue hardship.
- 2.3 Connect UHN's Accessibility Policy to the broader legislative framework.

### CLO 3: Analyze intersectionality in healthcare delivery

- 3.1 Explain how overlapping identity factors (race, gender, age, income, language) compound the impact of disability-related barriers.
- 3.2 Identify examples of intersectional barriers that patients and staff may experience within UHN settings.

### CLO 4: Differentiate reactive accommodation from proactive design

- 4.1 Describe the limitations of a case-by-case accommodation model in a large healthcare organization.
- 4.2 Explain how proactive, governance-based approaches to accessibility reduce institutional burden and improve dignity.
- 4.3 Apply the Accessibility Decision Path framework (Pause and Assess, Listen and Ask, Apply, Adapt, Seek Support) to a workplace scenario.

### CLO 5: Identify personal and team actions for shared responsibility

- 5.1 Articulate how accessibility responsibility extends beyond dedicated accessibility staff to every role at UHN.
- 5.2 Identify at least two concrete actions the learner can take in their own role to advance accessible practice.
- 5.3 Commit to a personal action using the My Action Planning (MAP) framework.

## 6. Assessment Strategy Summary

| Assessment Type | Purpose | Alignment | Placement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge Check 1** (multiple-choice, 2–3 items) | Confirm understanding of barrier types and disability models | CLO 1 | End of Module 2 |
| **Knowledge Check 2** (drag-and-drop matching) | Confirm understanding of AODA/OHRC obligations | CLO 2 | End of Module 3 |

**Grading approach:** This course uses a completion-based model. Knowledge checks provide formative feedback (correct/incorrect with rationale) but do not gate course completion. The MAP activity is required for completion.

**Passing criteria:** Learner must visit all required screens and complete the MAP activity to receive a completion status in the LMS.

## 8. Accessibility Considerations for This Course

### Content accessibility

- All text meets WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratio requirements (minimum 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text).
- Plain language is used throughout; reading level targets Grade 8 (Flesch-Kincaid).
- All acronyms (AODA, OHRC, UHN, IPAC, MAP) are spelled out on first use.
- Content avoids ableist language and models respectful, person-centred terminology.

### Media accessibility

- All images include meaningful alt text descriptions in the storyboard.
- All audio/video content includes synchronized captions and downloadable transcripts.
- No content is conveyed through colour alone.
- Animations can be paused or disabled; no auto-playing media.

### Interaction accessibility

- All interactions are keyboard-navigable (Tab, Enter, Arrow keys, Escape).
- Drag-and-drop activities include an accessible alternative (e.g., dropdown list selection).
- Focus indicators are visible and meet WCAG 2.1 AA requirements.
- Screen reader compatibility is tested with JAWS and NVDA before launch.
- Custom interactions include ARIA labels and role attributes.

### Storyline-specific considerations

- Player is configured with accessible player controls enabled.
- Tab order is set manually on each slide.
- Slide titles are meaningful and unique for screen reader navigation.
- Closed captions are embedded in Storyline (not burned into video).
- Course is tested with Storyline's built-in accessibility checker before publishing.

### Representational considerations

- Illustrations and scenario imagery reflect the diversity of UHN's patient and employee populations (race, age, gender, disability, cultural background).
- Scenarios avoid reinforcing stereotypes about disability.
- The course itself models the accessible practices it teaches.

## 10. Prerequisites

**Formal prerequisites:** None. This is Guide 1 of 18 and serves as the foundational course for the entire Accessibility First Series. No prior accessibility training is required.

**Recommended prior experience:** None. The course is designed for learners at all levels of accessibility awareness, from those encountering these concepts for the first time to those seeking to formalize existing knowledge.

**Note:** Completion of Guide 01 is recommended before proceeding to Guides 02–18, as it establishes shared language, frameworks (Accessibility Decision Path, Accessibility in Practice, Guiding Principles), and foundational concepts that are referenced throughout the series.

## 11. Production Notes

### Shared frameworks

This course introduces three frameworks that recur across all 18 guides. Ensure consistent visual treatment, terminology, and interaction patterns:

1. **Accessibility Decision Path** (5 steps): Pause and Assess, Listen and Ask, Apply, Adapt, Seek Support
2. **Accessibility in Practice** (4 quadrants): Awareness, Communication, Environment, Response
3. **Guiding Principles** (5 principles): Dignity, Independence/Autonomy, Inclusion/Participation, Proactive Barrier Prevention/Removal, Equity/Rights/Intersectionality

### Scenarios

Three scenarios are specified in the source content. Each requires storyboard development:

1. **Policy vs. Practice** — Branching scenario exploring the gap between written policy and daily practice
2. **Physical Environment** — Scenario highlighting environmental barriers in a healthcare setting
3. **Employee Awareness** — Scenario exploring how individual awareness (or lack thereof) affects accessibility

### Visual and media direction

- Illustration style should follow the UHN Accessibility Course Illustration Style System (see `/03-supporting-documents/ILLUSTRATION_STYLE_SYSTEM.md`).
- Use inclusive, representative illustrations generated via approved tools (NanoBanana, Midjourney, or GPT Image 2). Do not use Canva for image generation.
- Photography, if used, should reflect UHN's diverse patient and employee populations.

### Branding

- Follow UHN brand guidelines for colour palette, typography, and logo usage.
- Course shell should use UHN-approved Storyline template or a custom template aligned with UHN brand standards.

### Technical specifications

- Output: Articulate Storyline 360 (.story source file) and published SCORM 2004 package
- LMS compatibility: Tested against UHN's LMS environment
- Browser support: Chrome, Edge, Firefox (current versions); Safari (current version for macOS/iOS)
- Responsive: Storyline responsive player enabled for tablet/desktop; mobile support as feasible

**SME review gates:**

3. **Gate 3 — Final build review:** SME reviews the built Storyline course for content accuracy before LMS testing.
