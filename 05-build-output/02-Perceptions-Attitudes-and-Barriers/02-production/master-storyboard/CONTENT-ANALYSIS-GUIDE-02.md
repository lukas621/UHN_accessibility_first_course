## 4. Canadian / Ontario Legislative Context

| Source | Relevance to Guide 02 |
|---|---|
| **AODA** (Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005) | Mandates disability awareness training; attitudinal barriers addressed under Integrated Accessibility Standards (IAS) |
| **OHRC Human Rights Code** | Prohibits discrimination based on disability, mental health; duty to accommodate to point of undue hardship |
| **UN CRPD** (Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities) | Article 8 — awareness-raising; Article 3 — dignity, non-discrimination |
| **Ontario's Anti-Racism Act** | Intersects with attitudinal barriers and racialized disability experiences |
| **TRC Calls to Action** | Calls 18–24 address health equity for Indigenous Peoples — relevant to this guide's Indigenous content |

## 7. Voice and Tone Notes

- Guide 02 is more **reflective and emotionally resonant** than Guide 01 (which was primarily definitional)
- Content involves sensitive topics: mental health stigma, cultural assumptions, Indigenous history
- Narration should feel **warm, non-judgmental, and self-aware** — "we all have bias" framing
- Avoid language that implies only certain staff types need this training
- Use **Canadian English** spelling throughout (behaviour, honour, recognise)
- Scenario characters should reflect Toronto's diverse healthcare workforce (South Asian, Black, Indigenous, East Asian, White representation across staff and patient characters)

## 8. Accessibility Design Notes

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Scenario images | Person-first visual representation; no tokenistic imagery |
| Bias discussion | No imagery that could reinforce the very stereotypes being challenged |
| Indigenous content | Indigenous Peoples as agents, not victims — consult with UHN's Indigenous health team before final approval |
| Mental health scenario | Language aligned with CAMH anti-stigma guidelines |
| Cultural scenario | Avoid specific cultural markers in images that could feel stereotyped |
| All interactions | WCAG 2.1 AA minimum; keyboard navigable; screen reader compatible |
