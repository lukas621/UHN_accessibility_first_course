## Global Image Rules

- NO logos of any kind (including UHN, hospital names, brand marks)
- NO text overlays, signs with readable text, name badges with text, or any legible writing
- NO tokenistic or caricatured disability representation
- Disability represented with dignity — people are active agents, not subjects of pity
- Racial and cultural diversity: reflect Toronto's demographic reality across all images
- Healthcare setting: modern, well-lit, Canadian hospital aesthetic (not American)
- Lighting: warm natural light preferred; avoid harsh fluorescent look
- Style: photojournalistic — real moments, not posed stock photography
- Clothing: clinical scrubs, business casual, patient gowns — consistent with UHN environment
- Format: 16:9 landscape for hero/scenario images; square (1:1) available for icon/card use

## Image 08 — Attitudinal Barriers Accordion (Screen 2.8)

**File name:** g02-infographic-barriers-accordion-01.png
**Dimensions:** 800 x 1000 (portrait — or 6 individual icons at 200x200)
**Placement:** Screen 2.8 — accordion interaction, one icon per item

**GPT Image 2 Prompt (6 flat vector icons, minimal style, navy on white):**

- **Paternalism:** A hand reaching out to take something from another hand that is not extended — suggesting action without consent
- **Stereotyping:** Multiple identical figures with one highlighted or circled as "different" — an assumption imposed on an individual
- **Pity/Charity Model:** A figure looking downward at another smaller figure — implying hierarchy of sympathy
- **Spread Effect:** One connected node branching to multiple other nodes — one difference assumed to affect everything
- **Othering:** One figure standing apart from a group with a dotted boundary circle around them
- **Invisibilization:** A figure with a speech bubble that has a line through it — being seen but not heard or believed

**Alt text (per icon):**
- Paternalism: "Hand reaching toward another without consent — representing action without asking"
- Stereotyping: "Group of figures with one highlighted — representing generalization applied to an individual"
- Pity model: "Figure looking down at smaller figure — representing charity framing of disability"
- Spread effect: "Single node connecting to many — representing assumption that one disability affects all areas"
- Othering: "Figure separated from group by dotted boundary — representing exclusion and difference"
- Invisibilization: "Speech bubble with line through it — representing disclosure that is dismissed"
