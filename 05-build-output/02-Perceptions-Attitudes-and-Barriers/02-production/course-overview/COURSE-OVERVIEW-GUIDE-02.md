## Audience

**Primary audience:** All UHN staff — clinical and non-clinical, including:
- Nurses and allied health professionals
- Physicians and residents
- Administrative and support staff
- Managers and team leaders
- Environmental services and patient transport
- Volunteers and students on placement

**Prerequisites:**
- Guide 01: Foundations of Disability, Inclusion, and Accessible Design (required — Stage 1 prerequisite)
- No disability-specific expertise required
- No prior training in bias or reflective practice assumed

**Language:** English (French translation planned for future release)

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Define attitudinal barriers** and explain how implicit and explicit bias affect accessibility and equity in healthcare.

2. **Recognize assumptions** about ability, culture, mental health, and identity — and identify how these assumptions influence patient care and employee interactions at UHN.

3. **Apply reflective practice strategies** — using the Notice-Name-Examine-Act cycle — to identify and mitigate personal bias in everyday workplace interactions.

4. **Demonstrate proactive, dignity-centred responses** that move accessibility practice beyond compliance and toward genuine inclusion.
