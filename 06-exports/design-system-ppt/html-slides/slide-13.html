SECTION 12 · BRANCHING DECISION ACTIVITY

Choose the best accessibility response

Three first-step options. One is recommended. Each opens a different consequence panel -- then loops back to the Accessibility Decision Path.

SCENARIO · INTAKE

The patient is frustrated trying to complete the intake form.

You don't yet know whether the barrier is vision, language, digital access, cognitive load, or something else. The lobby is busy and three people are waiting behind them.

Time on task

3 minutes

DECISION · FIRST STEP

What do you do next, before assuming the cause?

Pick the option that best reflects "Pause & Assess" -- the first step of the Accessibility Decision Path.

A

Continue with the standard intake process.

Keep working through the form so the line keeps moving.

NEEDS IMPROVEMENT

B

Pause and ask how to support the patient.

"Is there anything I can do to make this form easier today?"

RECOMMENDED

C

Ask another staff member to take over.

Hand off to a colleague while you keep the queue moving.

SELECTED

FEEDBACK FOR B · RECOMMENDED

This is the dignified first move.

Pausing and asking, before assuming, is the single behaviour that distinguishes accessible care from "trying our best". The patient names the barrier -- vision, language, digital, cognitive, or something else -- and you act on what they actually need.
