Why Brewing Safety Matters

🍵

Every year, thousands of food service workers are injured by burns, scalds, and slip-and-fall incidents. Coffee brewing environments involve temperatures exceeding 200°F (93°C), pressurized steam, and wet surfaces.

Most injuries are preventable. A 2023 industry study found that 78% of barista injuries occurred when standard safety procedures were not followed.

Understanding the hazards in your workspace isn't just about compliance — it's about protecting yourself and your coworkers every single shift.

KEY TAKEAWAY

The majority of brewing injuries are preventable through consistent application of safety procedures.

Four Key Hazards

Click each card to reveal details. All four must be explored before continuing.
01

🔥 Thermal Burns

Hot water, steam wands, and heated surfaces can cause severe burns.
Prevention: Always use insulated handles. Never reach across active burners. Keep steam wand pointed away from your body. Announce "hot behind" when carrying heated containers.
02

⚡ Electrical Hazards

Wet hands + electrical equipment = serious risk of shock.
Prevention: Dry hands before touching switches or plugs. Keep cords away from water. Report frayed cords immediately. Never use equipment with damaged wiring.
03

💧 Slips & Spills

Wet floors from spilled water, milk, or cleaning create fall hazards.
Prevention: Clean spills immediately. Use "wet floor" signs. Wear non-slip footwear. Keep walkways clear of cords and equipment.
04

🧪 Chemical Exposure

Cleaning agents and descaling solutions require careful handling.
Prevention: Read all labels before use. Wear gloves when handling chemicals. Never mix cleaning products. Store chemicals away from food prep areas.

Test Your Understanding

QUESTION 1 OF 1

A coworker spills hot water on the counter and it drips onto the floor. What should you do first?

A Continue working and clean it up when you get a chance
B Tell your manager about the spill
C Warn nearby coworkers, then clean the spill immediately
D Place a wet floor sign and move on to the next order
✓ Correct!

C is correct. The priority is safety: warn others who might step in the spill, then clean it immediately. Leaving it even briefly creates a slip hazard.

Why not the others?

A — Delaying creates an active hazard. Spills should be addressed immediately.

B — Reporting is good, but the spill needs immediate action. You can inform your manager after securing the area.

D — A sign helps but doesn't remove the hazard. The spill still needs to be cleaned promptly.
